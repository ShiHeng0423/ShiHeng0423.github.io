About Me

I’m a gameplay programmer and technical designer who enjoys working under the hood of games — building the systems, tools, and moment-to-moment interactions that make a world feel responsive and alive.

My main focus is turning design ideas into solid, shippable systems. I like thinking about how combat flows, how AI should react, how data should be structured, and how all of that fits into a clean game-state and architecture that the whole team can rely on.

I work across Unity, Unreal, and custom C++/OpenGL engines, and I enjoy both gameplay work (controllers, abilities, enemies, encounters) and building tools that make iteration faster for designers and artists. Whether it’s wiring up a prototype quickly or polishing a feature for a milestone, I care a lot about feel, clarity, and helping the team move faster.

Long-term, my goal is to help create game worlds where people from very different backgrounds can still find something they genuinely enjoy together — whether that’s a mechanic, a story beat, or just a really satisfying fight.

Background

I’m currently studying game development, where I’ve worked on everything from low-level engine programming to higher-level gameplay features. This includes building a custom C++/OpenGL engine for a 2.5D arena shooter, co-leading a cooperative action game, and experimenting with AI, tools, and level-flow systems in various prototypes.

I’ve also completed an internship in the game-education space, creating Unity projects that integrate micro:bit hardware for classroom use. Across team projects, solo work, and internships, I’ve grown comfortable moving between design discussions, debugging sessions, and hands-on implementation.

Outside of development, I enjoy dissecting games, watching how different players approach the same problem, and sketching ideas for systems or encounters that could make those moments even more fun.
