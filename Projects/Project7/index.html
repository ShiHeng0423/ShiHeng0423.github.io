FYP @ NYP (Nov 2020 – Feb 2021)

During my Final Year Project at Nanyang Polytechnic, our team worked on two major tasks in parallel while keeping the overall project organised and on track. Due to NDA restrictions, I cannot share full details, but here is a clear summary of my involvement and responsibilities.

1. Completing & Polishing a Client-Based Project

I contributed to the refinement and finalisation of a client-facing project by:

  • Reviewing and integrating updated assets.
  • Tracking changes across multiple iterations and maintaining file consistency.
  • Ensuring all documentation stayed accurate and aligned with development progress.

This taught me the importance of structured workflows, proper version control, and clear communication when collaborating in a production environment.

2. Kickstarting a New In-House Game IP

I simultaneously supported early development for a new in-house game IP, focusing on:

  • Writing clean, well-commented, and maintainable code for future handovers.
  • Ensuring readability and proper organisation across the codebase.
  • Collaborating with teammates to align technical systems with design direction.

This reinforced the importance of scalability, maintainability, and proper planning—especially for long-term or multi-batch projects.

Key Takeaways

  • Improved project scoping and planning abilities.
  • Strengthened teamwork and communication while juggling parallel tasks.
  • Greater appreciation for clean code, documentation, and workflow discipline.
